Smith: Master Customer Database

Smith was named after the Smithsonian -- it's our grand repository and source of truth for customer contact data.

Smith: Add Customer lets us collect 36 pieces of data per customer as well as an additional 23 custom quotes for specific lab tests. Bonus: the quote data is written to the Lab Billing App.

Smith also allows us to attach and upload multiple categories of customer files, and will create the customer folders on Google Drive if they don't already exist.

The screenshots show Smith being accessed from the Lab Billing app via a submenu.

RMA Application

Nickname: The Beast

The RMA Application was created to track the meter traffic in and out of the shop and the work that was performed on each meter, from Receive to Job Complete. We were thinking BIG when we thought of this application and it is incredibly full-featured, including details such as logging comments to the activity cells and sending detailed Job Complete emails to customers.

This was my first application written as a professional developer. My supervisor and I met every Thursday and increased the scope of the project - lol. Lessons learned. It was challenging as hell and I am really pleased with how this app turned out. On a personal note, it's kind of fun for me to look at this and see the evolution of my code.

It's been stable and in regular use in the shop ever since it's official kraken release party in Jan 2020.

GriffeyJr: Field Ticket PWA

Taking the field ticket to the next level, GriffeyJr is my project to turn the digital field ticket into a Heroku-hosted Node.js app and use Google APIs to execute CRUD operations and manage field ticket data held in a Google Sheets workbook sitting safe behind the iron curtain of our Google Domain security.

I thought it would be a fun challenge to combine Node and Google APIs and butt my head against security protocols and force myself to learn by having to work through the challenges created by tackling this project.

I think this kind of thing is fun, yes. : )
